Problems On Trains


  1. The distance traveled by a train to cross a pole or person is equal to the length of the train.

  2. The distance traveled by train when it crosses a platform is equal to the sum of the length of the train and length of the platform.

  3. When two trains are travelling in opposite directions at speeds V1 m/s and V2 m/s then their relative speed is the sum of their individual speeds (V1+V2) m/s.

  4. Two trains are travelling in the same direction at V1 m/s and V2 m/s where V1 > V2 then their relative speed will be equal to the difference between their individual speeds (V1-V2) m/s.

  5. When two trains of length X meters and Y meters are moving in opposite direction at V1 m/s and V2 m/s then the time taken by the trains to cross each other is (X+Y)/(V1+V2) sec.

  6. When two trains of length X meters and Y meters are moving in same direction at V1 and V2 where V1 > V2 then the time taken by the faster train to cross the slower train (X+Y)/(V1-V2) sec.

  7. When two trains X and Y start moving towards each other at the same time from points A and B and after crossing each other the train X reaches point B in a seconds and train Y reaches points A in b seconds,
    then Train X speed : Train Y speed = √(b) : √(a)


Formula to convert Km/hr into m/s :

  • 1 km is equal to 1000 meters
  • 1 hour is equal to 3600 seconds
  • 1 Km/hr is equal to 1000/3600 or 5/18 meter/sec or m/s
Example :
120 km/hr = 120 × 5/18 = 33.33 m/s.

Formula to convert m/s into Km/hr :

  • 1 meter is equal to 1/1000 km
  • 1 sec is equal to 1/3600 hours
  • 1 m/s is equal to (1/1000)/(1/3600) or 3600/1000 or 18/5 km/hr
Example :
33.33 m/s = 33.33 × 18/5 = 120 km/hr.
