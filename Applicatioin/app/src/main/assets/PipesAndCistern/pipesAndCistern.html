Pipes And Cisterns


Pipes and cisterns problems are almost the same as those of Time and work problems. Thus, if a pipe fills a tank in 5 hrs, then the pipe fills 1/5th of the tank in 1 hr. the only difference with Pipes and Cisterns problems is that there are outlets as well as inlets. Thus, there are agents (the outlets) which perform negative work too. The rest of the process is almost similar.

  • INLET : An inlet pipe is connected with a tank and it fills tank.
  • OUTLET : A outlet pipe is connected with a tank and it empties tank.

Concept

  1. If a pipe can fill a tank in m hrs, then the part filled in 1 hr =1/m.

  2. If a pipe can empty a tank in n hrs, then the part of the full tank emptied in 1 hr = 1/n .

  3. If a pipe can fill a tank in m hrs and the another pipe can empty the full tank in n hrs, then the net part filled in 1 hr, when both the pipes are opened =[1/m- 1/n]

  4. Time taken to fill the tank, when both the pipes are opened = mn/( n-m)

  5. If a pipe can fill a tank in m hrs and another can fill the same tank in n hrs, then the net part filled in 1 hr, when both the pipes are opened = [1/n + 1/m]

  6. If a pipe fills a tank in m hrs and another fills the same tank in n hrs, but a third one empties the full tank in p hrs, and all of them are opened together, the net part filled in 1 hr = [1/m+ 1/n-1/p]
