Test Of Divisibility


1. Divisibility By 2 :
A number is divisible by 2 if its unit digit is any of 0,2,4,6,8.


Example. 5678 is divisible by 2, while 4567 is not divisile by 2.


2. Divisibility By 3 :
A number is divisible by 3 only when the sum of its digits is divisible by 3.


Example 1. In the number 5655, the sum of digits = 21, which is divisible by 3, therefore 5655 is divisible by 3.
Example 2. In the number 36754, the sum of digits = 25, which is not divisible by 3, therefore 5655 is not divisible by 3.


3. Divisibility By 4 :
A number is divisible by 4 if the sum of its last two digits is divisible by 4.


Example 1. 6879376 is divisible by 4, since 76 is divisible by 4.
Example 2. 496138 is not divisible by 4, since 38 is not divisible by 4.


4. Divisibility By 5 :
A number is divisible by 5 only when its unit digit is 0 or 5.


Example. Each of the number 35645 and 56780 is divisible by 5.


5. Divisibility By 8 :
A number is divisible by 8 if the last 3 digits is divisible by 8 .


Example 1. In the number 4892192, the number formed by last 3 digits, namely 192 is divisible by 8, therefore 4892192 is divisible by 8.
Example 2. In the number 4567633, the number formed by last 3 digits, namely 633 is not divisible by 8, therefore 4567633 is not divisible by 8.


6. Divisibility By 9 :
A number is divisible by 9 only when the sum of its digits is divisible by 9.


Example 1. In the number 246591, the sum of digits = 27, which is divisible by 9, therefore 246591 is divisible by 9.

7. Divisibility By 10 :
A number is divisible by 10 only when its unit digit is 0 .


Example 1. 68793760 is divisible by 10, since its unit digit is 0.
Example 2. 496138 is not divisible by 10, since its unit digit is not 0.


8. Divisibility By 11 :
A number is divisible by 11 if the difference between the sum of its digits at odd places and the sum of its digits at even places is either 0 or a number divisible by 11.
