Ratio And Propotion


1. Ratio : The ratio of two quantities a and b in the same units, is the fraction a/b and we write it as a:b.

In the ratio a:b, we call a as the first term or antecodent and b, the second term or connsequent.

Example : The ratio 5:9 represents 5/9 with antecedent= 5, consequent= 9.

Rule : The multiplication or division of each term of a ratio by the same non-zero number does not affect the ratio.

Example :
4 : 5 = 8 : 10 = 12 : 15 etc. Also, 4:6=2:3.


2. PROPORTION : The equality of two ratos is called proportion.

If a:b=c:d, we write, a:b::c:d and we say that a,b,c,d are in proportion.

Here a and d are called extermes, while b and c are called mean terms,

Product of means = product of extremes.
Thus, a:b::c:d = (b*c)=(a*d).


3.
  1. Fourth Proportional : If a:b = c:d, then d is called the fourth proportional to a,b,c.

  2. (2) Third Proportional : If a:b = b:c, then c is called the third proportional to a and b.

  3. Mean Proportional : Mean proportional between a and b is root ab.


4.
  1. Comparison of Ratios :
    We say that (a : b) > (c : d) = a/b > c/d.

  2. Compounded Ratios :
    The compounded ratio of the ratios (a:b), (c:d),(e:f) is (ace:bdf).

5.
  1. Duplicate ratio of (a:b) is (a square:b square).
  2. Sub-duplicate ratio of (a:b) is (root a: root b).
  3. Triplicate ratio of (a:b) is (a cube : b cube).
  4. Sub-triplicate ratio of (a:b) is (a:b).
  5. if a/b = c/d, then a+b/a-b = c+d/c-d.(componendo and dividendo)


6. Variation :
  1. We say that x is directly proportional to y, if x = ky for some constant k and we write, x proportional y.

  2. We say that x is inversely proportional to y, if xy = k for some constant k and we write, x proportional 1/y.
